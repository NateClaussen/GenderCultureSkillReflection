Culture Observations by attending Redemption Church

Observations

I had the opportunity to attend another church for this assignment to experience the culture that I am not as familiar with. I would say that I had a good experience and found it fascinating the small differences that made the culture of this church different from the one I normally attend. Briefly stated, the first thing that I noticed was that the tone of the meeting felt more casual as the dress code was more business casual vs what I would consider a “Sunday best.” Right when I walked into the service, I noticed that everyone just stayed standing up through the music and stayed standing until the pastor came out to deliver their sermon. The meeting was very Bible and Christ Centered, and the congregation seemed to pay close attention to what was being taught.

Response

The habitus was actually very familiar to me as I myself am a Christian. As I was able to attend this Christian church, it did not surprise me that the things that were talked about, felt, and shared were already familiar things to me. There were differences in the way the meeting was ran that were different from what I was used to, but it helped me to learn that some cultures can be very similar to your own. I also learned that even though I may not be familiar with a culture, I can still find similarities that make me feel comfortable and welcomed.

Analysis

In the meeting that I attended, the meeting was about the churches core values and belief that they want to focus on. They specifically talked about multiplying in terms of service and helping the community as well as missionary work and expanding their church. I was also impressed with the love that everyone seemed to have for each other, and felt like the church had a culture of accepting those who are new. As seen in the picture, I was able to see one of the leaders at the church that I met on Weber State campus who originally invited me to their services. While I will stay strong in my current faith, I really value the opportunity that I had to experience this different culture and would even be open to going again. On the Culture Competence Continuum, I would say that this experience has put me in the “respect” category for these people.

Gender Observations from the movie: Megamind

Observations

Megamind is a movie that came out in 2010 which portrays two little alien babies’ different lives growing up on earth. One of them is very smart (Megamind) and the other can fly and has laser vision (Metro Man). Throughout the movie, there is also a main female character (Roxanne) who acts as a catalyst to move the story along. Throughout the movie, I noticed that out of the 5 main characters only one of them was a female. When Minion (the fish) acts as “Space Step Mom,” the way that they distinguish themselves as a female is with a wig and an apron implying that they follow the societal norms with being a female in the kitchen always providing good snacks.

Response

Roxanne, while fitting the gender role as a news reporter, develops a greater sense of character and breaks the stereotypes by being strong, independent, and not afraid to speak her mind. She is portrayed as very independent and not afraid to stand up for what she believes in. The example of Roxanne is showing that it is acceptable for both males and females to have their own opinion and to break the mold that society has put on them. The example of Minion being “space step mom” almost juxtaposes the example of Roxanne, because it shows minion in an apron always providing treats and being more of the stereotypical “kitchen women.” Both of these portrayals are neither a good or bad thing; rather, they allow the viewer of the film to think critically about the message of the movie.

Analysis

Overall, I have learned a lot watching this movie through the lens of a student observing gender in society. I can see that the way that women are being portrayed in this movie shows that both men and women can have success. While the theme of the movie is good vs evil, the way that Megamind is able to go from evil to good shows that you do not have to be stuck where you are at. You can change and do not always have to be evil. Without the distinct roles of male and female in the movie, the message being portrayed would not have been the same.
